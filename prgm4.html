Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

Types of AI

  • Narrow or Weak AI: Designed to perform a specific task, such as facial recognition or language translation.
  • General or Strong AI: Designed to perform any intellectual task that a human can, such as reasoning, problem-solving, and learning.
  • Superintelligence: Significantly more intelligent than the best human minds, potentially leading to significant technological advancements.

Applications of AI

  • Virtual Assistants: AI-powered assistants, such as Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant, can perform tasks, answer questions, and provide information.
  • Image and Speech Recognition: AI-powered systems can recognize and interpret images, speech, and text, enabling applications such as self-driving cars and language translation.
  • Healthcare: AI can help diagnose diseases, develop personalized treatment plans, and streamline clinical workflows.
